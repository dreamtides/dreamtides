# Dreamtides Resonance

# Introduction

The Dreamtides “resonance” system is intended to provide a readable visual
signal to players to inform their drafting choices. Ultimately this system is
cosmetic: resonance does not affect what cards can be played in decks. However,
it still provides a valuable framework for thinking about card design and
helping players understand how cards might relate to each other, while the
natural pressures of a competitive drafting game tend to push players into decks
built around a single resonance.

The four resonance types form a circle, and each resonance has two “archetypes”
which form a second circle. We say that two adjacent archetypes have an
“alliance”, meaning that cards for that archetype should be playable in their
allied archetype as well.

# Stone

Stone is the resonance of inevitability through attrition: what breaks is still
useful, and what is spent comes back transformed. It asks players to treat the
void as an extension of hand and battlefield. Mechanically, Stone turns
dissolves, abandons, and reclaim loops into long-game engines that outlast
faster decks. Its core play pattern is converting short-term loss into durable
advantage. Its weaknesses are slow starts and dependence on the void staying
accessible.

**Favored Character Type**: Survivors

## Endure

The Endure archetype is all about the void. It is centered around the “Survivor”
character type: characters that specialize in interacting with the void and the
reclaim mechanic. Endure fills its void quickly and then brings them back for
value. This is also the home of “reanimator” decks that bring a strong character
into play from the void.

Endure is allied with Submerge and Shatter. We should try to support these
archetypes by having discard and abandon as costs or effects.

## Alliance: Endure and Shatter

Endure is allied with Shatter because of the natural overlap in their interests:
Endure wants things in the void, Shatter puts them there. Endure is also great
at repeatedly bringing back characters to serve as fuel for Shatter’s abandon
engine.

## Shatter

The Shatter archetype centers around the abandon mechanic, dissolving its own
characters for value. It is also the home of “cheat into play” abilities,
letting it bypass energy costs for expensive cards.

Shatter is allied with Endure and Ignite. We should try to support these
archetypes with effects that fill the void and by supporting warriors and
figments.

## Alliance: Shatter and Ignite

Shatter is allied with Ignite, the archetype with the greatest ability to flood
the board with characters, which in turn can be turned into resources by
abandoning them.

# Flame

Flame is the resonance of pressure, momentum, and forcing answers now. It wants
the game decided by initiative, not inevitability, by turning every turn into a
question the opponent must answer immediately. Mechanically, Flame emphasizes
go-wide board presence, figment generation, and explosive materialized payoffs
that reward committing to the battlefield. Its weaknesses are overextension and
running out of resources if pressure stalls.

**Favored Character Type:** Warriors

## Ignite

Ignite is the premier “go wide” aggro archetype. It specializes in producing
lots of characters and then pumping them up for value. The “Warrior” card type
is at home here, as well as the ability to create “Figment” character tokens.

Ignite is allied with Shatter and Flicker. We should support these archetypes
with payoffs for abandoning characters and making sure warriors are a strong
“materialized trigger” tribe.

## Alliance: Ignite and Flicker

Ignite is allied with Flicker because it gets value from repeating its
“materialized” triggers to support the go-wide strategy, with an engine based on
repeating cards that create figments or pump up warriors.

## Flicker

Flicker is the “value” archetype with characters that create card advantage over
time. Its central strategy is the “banish and then materialize” loop, which
re-triggers the “materialized” ability of characters. It also has the ability to
banish enemy characters temporarily to let it get ahead. Flicker is also a
natural home for control decks, since its strong value engines let it stay ahead
into the late game when it can win with a big finisher.

Flicker is allied with Ignite and Awaken. We should support these archetypes by
putting Flicker effects on Warriors and Spirit Animals and by making sure we can
use the energy generated by Awaken.

## Alliance: Flicker and Awaken

Flicker is allied with Awaken because it needs the ability to generate a lot of
energy to transition into the late game. Awaken’s Spirit Animals often provide
the big win condition for a focused Flicker deck.

# Thunder

Thunder is the resonance of growth and scale: invest early, dominate late, and
make each turn's energy matter more than the last. It frames energy as a power
curve to climb, then cashes that curve into oversized threats and high-impact
plays. Mechanically, Thunder focuses on ramp, efficient energy conversion, and
go-tall board states that demand precise answers. Its gameplay should reward
planning turns ahead and sequencing around big inflection points. Its weakness
is tempo loss when early setup is disrupted.

**Favored Character Type:** Spirit Animals

## Awaken

Thunder is all about “ramp”: getting more energy than the opponent and winning
through sheer size. It is the home of the “Spirit Animal” tribe. Its characters
produce energy when materialized during the Judgment phase and can be leveraged
in huge threats.

Awaken is allied with Flicker and Flash. We should support these archetypes by
having lots of “fast” Spirit Animal payoffs as well as producing value via
“materialized” triggers for the Flicker deck.

## Alliance: Awaken and Flash

Awaken and Flash are allied because they both benefit from the “go tall”
strategy. Many of Awaken’s characters can be played at fast speed, and Awaken
supports the idea of investing resources into a single character and then
protecting it.

## Flash

Flash is the “tempo” archetype that cares about playing a cheap threat and then
protecting it. It has payoffs for playing “fast” speed cards, as well as a “go
tall” focus, pumping up a single character and then keeping it safe via
counterspells.

Flash is allied with Awaken and Surge. We should support them by making sure we
help consume the energy produced by Awaken and channel it into a big threat,
while providing protection and a redundant win condition for Surge.

## Alliance: Flash and Surge

Flash and Surge are allied because they are the two archetypes that care most
about events. Surge can benefit from the protection/counterspells provided by
Flash to help secure a win during its combo turn, producing a “Combo-Control”
deck. The Flash player can also put together wins by chaining together Surge
events with a character that grows bigger when events are played.

# Tide

Tide is the resonance of flow, precision, and cadence. It is less about raw
board size and more about chaining events, sculpting hands, and timing the right
burst turn. Mechanically, Tide leans on discard/filtering, events-matter
payoffs, and stack interaction to convert sequencing skill into advantage. Its
signature wins come from assembling critical turns where many small effects
compound into a decisive point swing or combo finish. Its weaknesses are high
setup demands that make it fragile to early pressure.

**Favored Character Type:** Musicians

## Surge

Surge is the preeminent “events matter” archetype, and it tends to be the most
combo-focused of the primary 8. The Surge game plan is all about a “storm” win
condition, playing a large number of events in a single turn to generate a win.
Surge is also the archetype most directly able to generate victory points
outside of the Judgment phase, giving it a “Burn” subtheme.

Surge is allied with Flash and Submerge. The protection provided by Flash helps
with the combo turn, while we can support Flash with many of the “events matter”
cards that can be played for value. Surge has natural synergy with the discard
focus of the Submerge archetype since it needs to filter through its deck
quickly to put together the pieces for a win, and can help the Submerge player
by providing a reliable card advantage engine to power their discards.

## Alliance: Surge and Submerge

Surge and Submerge form a natural pairing of archetypes because the Surge player
often wins the game through “wheel” effects, discarding cards to draw more
cards, as well as just generally filtering its deck via discard. The Submerge
archetype also plays a lot of powerful event cards, and can often get value from
things like the event doubling and one-shot energy generation that makes Surge
so powerful.

## Submerge

Submerge is the “discard matters” archetype. The primary plan here is discarding
your own cards for value, either through filling up your void or more directly
through “when you discard” triggers. Submerge also has access to the ability to
discard the opponent’s cards to disrupt their game plan, and is generally the
center of most “midrange” strategies, playing efficient interaction that stops
the opponent’s strategy and then winning with high-value threads.

Submerge is allied with Surge and Endure. We can support these archetypes by
helping the Surge player execute their win condition by filtering their draws,
as well as providing the “event cards in your void matter” plan. We can help
Endure players by giving them a way to get high-value cards into the void that
they can reclaim.

## Alliance: Submerge and Endure

Submerge and Endure are allied through shared void infrastructure: Submerge
loads the void efficiently, while Endure converts that stockpile into repeatable
material and reclaim value. Together they form a resilient midrange-control
shell that trades resources early and wins on recursion density later. This
alliance should reward cards that both discard and recur, plus payoffs for
specific card types or thresholds in your void.
